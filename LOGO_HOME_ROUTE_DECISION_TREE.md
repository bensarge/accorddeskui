## Brand-Focused Options

**Your priority:** Logo should be prominent and immediately visible

### Question: Should the logo be in the sidebar or content area?

#### **Sidebar** → Choose between:

**Option 1: Logo Above Sidebar Title (100px)**
- ✅ Best for: Strong brand presence with clear hierarchy
- ✅ Logo is first element users see
- ✅ Maintains "Drafting Manager" text
- ⚠️ Takes vertical space in sidebar

**Option 2: Logo Replaces Sidebar Title (120px)**
- ✅ Best for: Logo-first design, minimalist sidebar
- ✅ Largest logo size in sidebar
- ✅ Removes text redundancy
- ⚠️ Assumes logo is self-explanatory

**Option 7: Dual Placement (60px + 50px)**
- ✅ Best for: Maximum visibility in both areas
- ✅ Reinforced branding throughout
- ✅ Logo always visible
- ⚠️ May feel repetitive

#### **Content Area** → Choose:

**Option 5: Centered Above Template Library (90px)**
- ✅ Best for: Eye-catching content area branding
- ✅ Creates "welcome" feeling
- ✅ Professional presentation
- ⚠️ Scrolls away with content

## Functional Options

**Your priority:** Logo should be subtle and not interfere with navigation

### Question: How subtle should the branding be?

#### **Subtle but Visible** → Choose:

- ✅ Logo as supporting element
- ✅ Doesn't interfere with navigation
- ✅ Small but noticeable

**Option 6: Logo at Sidebar Bottom (70px)**
- ✅ Best for: Footer-like placement
- ✅ Non-intrusive
- ✅ Always visible (doesn't scroll)
- ✅ Professional appearance

- ✅ Best for: No functional space used
- ✅ Elegant, premium feel
- ✅ Modern design aesthetic
- ✅ Maintains full sidebar functionality
- ⚠️ Very subtle - may not be noticed

## Use Case Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Brand-New Application Launch
**Goal:** Establish strong brand recognition

**Recommended:** 
- Primary: **Option 1 (Above Sidebar Title)** or **Option 2 (Replaces Title)**
- Alternative: **Option 7 (Dual Placement)**

**Reasoning:** New users need to quickly recognize and remember your brand.

---

### Scenario 2: Established Application with Known Brand
**Goal:** Maintain brand presence without being intrusive

**Recommended:**
- Primary: **Option 3 (Corner Logo)** or **Option 6 (Sidebar Bottom)**
- Alternative: **Option 8 (Watermark)**

**Reasoning:** Users already know your brand; subtle reinforcement is sufficient.

---

### Scenario 3: Premium/Enterprise Product
**Goal:** Convey sophistication and professionalism

**Recommended:**
- Primary: **Option 8 (Sidebar Watermark)**
- Alternative: **Option 1 (Above Sidebar Title)** with refined styling

**Reasoning:** Premium aesthetic with elegant, non-intrusive branding.

---

### Scenario 4: Content-Heavy Application
**Goal:** Keep focus on content, not navigation

**Recommended:**
- Primary: **Option 4 (Main Header)** or **Option 5 (Centered Main)**
- Alternative: **Option 6 (Sidebar Bottom)** for subtle sidebar presence

**Reasoning:** Logo associated with content area; sidebar stays functional.

---

### Scenario 5: Multi-Section Application
**Goal:** Logo should adapt to different sections

**Recommended:**
- Primary: **Option 4 (Main Header)**
- Alternative: **Option 7 (Dual Placement)**

**Reasoning:** Logo in content area can change per section; sidebar stays consistent.

---

### Scenario 6: Mobile-First Application
**Goal:** Optimize for mobile experience

**Recommended:**
- Primary: **Option 3 (Corner Logo)** or **Option 6 (Sidebar Bottom)**
- Alternative: **Option 8 (Watermark)**

**Reasoning:** Space-efficient options that work well when sidebar becomes full-width on mobile.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ **Don't:** Use dual placement unless brand presence is critical
- Can feel repetitive and cluttered

❌ **Don't:** Replace sidebar title if logo doesn't include brand name
- Users may not understand what section they're in

❌ **Don't:** Use watermark if brand recognition is low
- Too subtle for new or unknown brands

❌ **Don't:** Put logo in main header if content scrolls frequently
- Logo will disappear from view

❌ **Don't:** Use large logo in sidebar if navigation is complex
- Takes space needed for navigation items

---

## Final Checklist

Before making your final decision, verify:

- [ ] Logo is visible and recognizable at chosen size
- [ ] Logo doesn't interfere with primary navigation
- [ ] Logo works on both dark sidebar and light content backgrounds
- [ ] Logo placement aligns with brand strategy (prominent vs. subtle)
- [ ] Logo is accessible to screen readers
- [ ] Logo placement works on mobile devices
- [ ] Logo doesn't create visual clutter
- [ ] Logo enhances rather than distracts from user tasks
